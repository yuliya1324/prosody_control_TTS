Audio samples from " Text Markup for Prosody Control in Speech Synthesis"

Authors: Yuliya Korotkova, Ilya Kalinovsky.

Abstract: Modern Text-to-Speech (TTS) technologies generate speech very close to the natural one, but synthesized voices still lack variation in intonation which, in addition, is hard to control. In this work we address the problem of prosody control aiming to capture information about intonation in a markup without hand labeling and linguistic expertise. We propose a method of encoding prosodic knowledge from textual and acoustic modalities obtained with the help of models pretrained on Self-Supervised tasks into latent quantized space with interpretable features. Based on these features, the prosodic markup is constructed, and it is used as an additional input to the TTS model in order to solve the one-to-many problem and is predicted by text. Moreover, this method allows prosody control on inference time and scalability to new data and other languages.

Comparison of different TTS architectures

These examples show the influence of the proposed markup on different TTS architectures.

Baseline: model trained without prosodic markup.

Ground Truth: model trained with prosodic markup, and samples are generated with ground truth labels from the database.

Text Prediction: model trained with prosodic markup, and samples are generated with labels predicted by text.
